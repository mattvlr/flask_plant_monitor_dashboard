1) Overview

Peppers (Capsicum annuum/chinense/baccatum) are day‑length tolerant and fruit indoors if light intensity, temperature, and airflow are balanced. A single 600 W LED can sustain 2–6 plants depending on canopy size, cultivar vigor, and target PPFD.

  • Goal: 20–30 mol·m⁻²·day⁻¹ DLI to maintain flowering, with leaf temps ~24–28 °C.
  • Key risks: Low PPFD/DLI, stagnant air, excessive heat at apical buds, and chronic overwatering.
  • Levers: Light height/dimmer, fan placement, pot volume, and steady EC.

2) Variety Profiles

Annumm: Jalapeño / Serrano / Bell

  • Habit: Compact to medium; responsive to topping.
  • PPFD: 400–500.
  • Pot: 3–5 gal per plant for indoor tents.
  • Notes: Fast bloom; reliable set under 55–60 % RH.

Chinense: Habanero / Scotch Bonnet

  • Habit: Bushy, slower veg; likes warmth.
  • PPFD: 450–600.
  • Pot: 5–7 gal recommended.
  • Notes: Sensitive to cool nights; delay set if < 20 °C.

Superhots: Ghost / Reaper / 7‑Pot

  • Habit: Tall, apically dominant; longer time‑to‑ripe.
  • PPFD: 500–650 if heat managed.
  • Pot: 7–10 gal to stabilize moisture.
  • Notes: Keep VPD gentle during early flowering to avoid bud drop.

Baccatum: Aji types

  • Habit: Open, branching; tall with arching laterals.
  • PPFD: 450–550.
  • Pot: 5–7 gal; trellis early.
  • Notes: Fragrant flowers; benefits from 15–16 h photoperiod.

4) Lighting with a 600W LED

Photoperiod and Intensity

  • Baseline: 14–16 h photoperiod year‑round.
  • Dimmer/height: Target canopy PPFD 400–600. Use a PAR meter or spot‑check with reference map.
  • Heat: Verify leaf temp with IR thermometer. LED heat can still raise leaf temp near diodes.

Mounting Heights (starting points)

PPFD TargetTypical Height*Use
250–30060–75 cmSeedling/early root‑in
400–50045–60 cmVeg and early flower
550–65035–50 cmBulk ripening if heat controlled

*Actual height depends on fixture optics and dimmer setting. Confirm with PPFD readouts.

Canopy Management

  • Flatten canopy via topping once at 5–7 nodes; then low‑stress tie‑outs.
  • Keep apical buds 5–10 cm below the light’s hotspot to reduce bud drop.
  • Rotate pots weekly for uniformity.

Airflow

  • Inline exhaust near light; passive intake opposite low side.
  • Clip fans at canopy height for gentle flutter. No hurricane winds.
  • Filter inlet with prefilter to reduce pests/dust.

7) Watering, Media, and Fertility

Media

  • Soilless peat/coco mixes drain well and accept precise feed.
  • Add 20–30 % perlite for oxygenation.
  • Fabric pots improve root air‑prune and drying uniformity.

Watering

  • Water when top 2–3 cm dry. Avoid constant saturation.
  • Aim for 10–20 % runoff in fertigation sessions to avoid salt buildup.

8) Pruning, Training, and Spacing

  • Top once at 5–7 nodes to create a low, wide structure.
  • Use soft ties to open the center for airflow and light.
  • Remove the lowest leaf pair after canopy fills to reduce humidity pockets.
  • Stake or trellis before fruit load to prevent splits.

9) Pollination and Fruit Set

  • Maintain RH 50–60 % during open flowers for viable pollen.
  • Daily gentle shake or a small oscillation cycle improves pollination.
  • Small brush or electric toothbrush on the stem can supplement on superhots.

Prevention

  • Inspect new plants before entry. Quarantine 7–10 days if possible.
  • Keep floor dry. Remove leaf litter.
  • Run intake through filter. Clean fans monthly.

11) Sensors & Automation

Useful Sensors

  • PAR/PPFD meter: tune dimmer/height to targets.
  • Temp/RH sensors at canopy and root zone.
  • Soil moisture probes per pot.
  • Lux sensor for relative light stability checks.
  • Camera for time‑lapse and growth tracking.

Automation Ideas

  • Fan speed tied to RH or leaf temp thresholds.
  • Irrigation timers with moisture‑based overrides.
  • Alert on RH > 70 % during flowering.

12) Daily/Weekly Schedules

Daily

  • AM: Check canopy temp/RH, inspect buds, confirm fan operation.
  • Midday: Verify PPFD at canopy; rotate pots if needed.
  • PM: Water if pot mass indicates; log runoff EC if fertigating.

Weekly

  • Wipe tent walls and light bars. Replace yellow sticky cards.
  • Light training adjustments; re‑tie branches.
  • Flush with plain water if EC climbs above target.

13) Troubleshooting

Low Yield

  • Increase PPFD to 500–600 if temps allow.
  • Reduce RH to 50–55 % during peak flower.
  • Check Ca/Mg levels and steady irrigation cadence.

Leaf Burn

  • Raise light or dim 10–20 % until margins recover.
  • Verify that runoff EC is not excessive.

14) Harvest & Postharvest

  • Pick green jalapeños for crunch; allow color for sweeter heat.
  • Superhots require full color and waxy firmness; be patient.
  • Do not strip the plant bare at once; stagger harvest for continued set.

Appendix A: Power & Cost

Formulas

Daily kWh ≈ (Wattage × hours) ÷ 1000
Monthly kWh ≈ Daily kWh × 30
Monthly cost ≈ Monthly kWh × local $/kWh

Example (600 W @ 16 h)

  • Daily kWh ≈ 0.6 × 16 = 9.6 kWh
  • Monthly kWh ≈ 9.6 × 30 = 288 kWh
  • At $0.12/kWh → ≈ $34.56 per month

Substitute your local rate.
